## Your Role and Responsibilities

An AI Engineer at IBM is not just a job title — it's a mindset. You'll leverage the watsonx platform to co-create AI value with clients, focusing on technology patterns to enhance repeatability and delight clients. Your primary responsibilities will include:

- **Build pilots, prototypes, agent workflows for client PoC** — Engage in developing AI services and deploying AI platform to solve real-world business problems in various industries using the IBM watsonx platform. This involves covering all stages of the AI solution lifecycle and co-leading technical workshops with the sales team.
- **Cover the AI Solution Lifecycle** — Perform tasks across the AI solution lifecycle, including Data Engineering, Train, Validate, Tune, Deploy, AI Operations, and AI Governance to create a trusted end-to-end AI solution.

## Day-to-Day Duties

- **AI-Native PoC Development** — Design and implement AI-native Proofs of Concept (PoCs) and agentic workflows using platforms such as watsonx Orchestrate and IBM BoB to demonstrate tangible business value. Collaborate with product and cross-functional teams to develop scalable, repeatable solution patterns and rapidly iterate on architectures to accelerate adoption and long-term success.
- **Client Engagement and Technical Wins** — Engage closely with enterprise clients to identify strategic business priorities, technical challenges, and unmet needs, and convert them into actionable AI solution strategies that drive technical success and business impact.
- **Documentation and Knowledge Sharing** — Document solution architectures, key design decisions, implementation details, and lessons learned in a structured manner. Produce technical documentation, white papers, and best practice guides, while contributing to internal knowledge sharing and capability development across the organization.
- **Industry Trends and Innovation** — Stay up to date with the latest trends and advancements in Agentic AI, foundation models, and large language models. Evaluate emerging technologies, tools, and frameworks to assess their potential impact on solution design and implementation.
